My Video Project

The Idea

The video titled "Fifteen Minutes Around the Arts Center" captures a quiet yet profound exploration of the spaces that make up the Arts Center and its surroundings. With a blend of wide shots and close-ups, it reveals the architectural elegance of often-overlooked structures, like the boxy residential buildings and the serene palms. The simplicity of everyday moments is highlighted, showing the intricate beauty in what many might pass by without a second glance. Each scene conveys the unspoken significance these spaces hold, both as part of the Arts Center’s function and as elements of its ambiance. The film’s 15-minute constraint emphasizes the fleeting nature of time, urging viewers to appreciate the details and serenity found in even the most familiar settings. With minimal distractions, the video focuses on the essence of place, offering a meditative reflection on the purpose and role of the Arts Center in shaping not just the landscape, but the experiences and actions of those who inhabit it. The background music, "Resonance" by Home, mirrors the flow of the film with its smooth, rhythmic progression. The soothing, nostalgic vibe of "Resonance" reinforces the idea of finding beauty in the familiar, subtly guiding the viewer through the narrative with a mellow yet captivating presence.

About Me

Hello! I'm Haroon Shafi, I'm from Pakistan, and I'm currently a sophomore at New York University Abu Dhabi, majoring in Computer Science and minoring in Interactive Media and Applied Mathematics. As part of my "Communications Lab" course, I've made this website to showcase my video project, titled "Fifteen Minutes Around the Arts Center", featuring clips shot around the Arts Center in the span of 15 minutes (well, most of them :p)!
